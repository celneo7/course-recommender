"Taken in AY18/19 Sem 1 Lecturer: Asst Prof Harold Soh Synopsis: CS2040 introduces fundamental data structures and algorithms to students, as well as some basic asymptotic analysis. The module is taught in Java and assumes basic working knowledge of Java. Assessment: Comprised of weekly/fortnightly take-home problemsets (30%), two quizzes (6%), a coding quiz (4%), a midterm (10%), participation (10%) and a final exam (40%). Weekly/fortnightly problemsets are interesting and are for putting concepts into practice, and are not difficult to do well in, given that collaboration is encouraged and the long deadline. The two quizzes serve as evaluations of learning progress in the module and are not difficult with a good understanding of the module content. The coding quiz is a practical exam and there's a lot of time for you figure out what to do (the problemsets should prepare you for this). The midterm and finals do not focus on how exactly the data structures and algorithms work, but how they can be applied to solve various algorithmic problems, and so exposure to a variety of problems will help. Finally, in-class and forum participation are encouraged and will count towards the participation component of the final grade. Lecturer: Prof Harold is an excellent lecturer with a great flair for teaching. He engages the audience well and lectures are generally interesting, even if you have prior knowledge."
